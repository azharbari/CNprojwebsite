Unit 1, 1.1 Describing the purpose of IT in modern business

These days IT touches every single industry and business you can think of no matter how big or small. Some of the things modern businesses use IT for include advertising, securing, training employees, storing data, receiving and making payments. You as a consumer on a weekly basis may use the internet for purchasing something, ordering a takeaway delivery, paying bills and even hailing a taxi.

For example let's take a restaurant to describe the purpose of IT in modern business. The restaurant will need to advertise its business so they can broaden their audience. They can provide the audience with information such as their location, contact details, opening closing times and even allow them to make a seat reservation online. They can also extend this to creating an option for their audience to make an order online for delivery, taking payment for this and also providing feedback to promote the restaurants goodwill even further. The restaurant can also use IT to train the staff in food hygiene for example by getting them to complete an online course. It will also need to submit a tax return to HMRC which they may also do online and they may pay all the other bills online too. Therefore it can be seen that the purpose of IT in modern business is an integral and vital part to it and can make life more convenient for owners, employees and consumers a like.

1.1.4 Describe the risks that might impact digital projects

As in life things can always go wrong so it would be wise to discuss the risks that may impact digital projects. This could be external factors such as the damage of the physical tools such as damage to the laptop/PC, damage to the connection causing connectivity problems or the human falling ill or becoming injured. Any of these even alone let alone combined can cause a halt to the project because they are all individually vital to the completion of the project. Without the human who is going to control the project and work on it but without the laptop or internet connection what is the human going to control exactly.

Furthermore, internal problems can occur such as a virus or glitch within the system which can lead to a loss of data or a complete crash of the laptop/pc. These internal risks can be minimised by installing a competent antivirus software and keeping it updated combined with making sure to install the regular updates provided by your operating system provider. Moreover data should also be backed up to a cloud storage so that if ever the laptop/PC/physical storage was ever damaged for any reason the data lost can easily be accessed and downloaded from the cloud storage. Examples of cloud storage applications are Dropbox, iCloud and Amazon Web Services.

1.1.7 Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects

The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) is a legal framework that sets guidelines for the collection and processing of personal information from individuals who live in the EU. [1]   In particular it permanently changed the way you can collect, store and use data. Thus it can impact digital projects in that they would need to put in to place safeguards against the breaking of the rules set out by the GDPR as the punishments can be severe.

The digital projects would need to ensure that the data collected can be identified as to where it resides, who can access it and if there are any risks to the data. Furthermore it would need to made sure that irrelevant data for the purposes of the project are deleted and discarded as GDPR requires data held to be relevant to the projects purposes. Also the data would need to be kept secure by putting security measures in place to guard against data breaches, and taking quick action to notify individuals and authorities in the event a breach does occur [2].
